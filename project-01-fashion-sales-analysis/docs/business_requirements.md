# Business Requirements Document (BRD)
## TrendHive — Sales Performance Analysis

---

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Prepared By:** Erica (Junior Business Analyst)
**Date:** 2024
**Status:** Draft

---

## 1. Business Background

TrendHive is an Australian fashion retailer selling women's and men's clothing, accessories, and footwear. They operate 4 stores across Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Perth, as well as an online store.

The business has been collecting sales data for 6 months but has never formally analysed it. Leadership wants to understand performance before planning the next season's buying budget.

---

## 2. Business Problem

> *"We collect a lot of data but we don't know what's working and what isn't. We need someone to make sense of it."*
> — Sales Manager, TrendHive

**Pain Points:**
- No visibility into which product categories drive revenue
- No understanding of geographic performance differences
- No reporting tool for management to self-serve insights

---

## 3. Business Objectives

| # | Objective |
|---|---|
| 1 | Understand revenue by product category |
| 2 | Identify top performing stores/locations |
| 3 | Spot seasonal trends in customer buying behaviour |
| 4 | Identify the top 5 products to restock |
| 5 | Provide management with a visual dashboard |

---

## 4. Scope

**In Scope:**
- 6 months of transaction-level sales data
- 4 physical store locations + online channel
- Product categories: Tops, Bottoms, Dresses, Accessories, Footwear

**Out of Scope:**
- Customer demographics analysis
- Supplier or cost data
- Future forecasting

---

## 5. Stakeholders

| Name | Role | Interest |
|---|---|---|
| Sales Manager | Primary Stakeholder | Wants summary of performance |
| Buying Team | Secondary | Wants to know what products to reorder |
| Finance Team | Secondary | Wants revenue figures by region |

---

## 6. Deliverables

- [ ] Cleaned sales dataset (CSV)
- [ ] SQL database with sales tables
- [ ] SQL queries answering the 5 business questions
- [ ] Power BI dashboard with 4-5 visuals
- [ ] Written insights report (plain English summary)

---

## 7. Key Questions (to answer with data)

1. Which product category generates the most revenue?
2. Which store location has the highest total sales?
3. What are the top 5 best-selling products by quantity?
4. Which month had the highest revenue?
5. What is the average order value across all transactions?

---

## ✏️ Learning Note

A Business Requirements Document (BRD) is one of the most important documents a Business Analyst writes. Before any data work begins, you first need to understand:
- **WHY** the business needs this analysis
- **WHO** needs it
- **WHAT** questions need answering

Practice writing BRDs for every project — it shows employers you think like an analyst, not just a data technician.
